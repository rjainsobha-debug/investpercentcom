10 Costly Mistakes New Investors Make

Every mistake an investor makes has been made millions of times before. The good news: knowing these mistakes in advance means you don't have to pay the tuition fee of experience.

Mistake #1

Starting "When the Market Is Right"

There is no right time. The market is always either "too high" or "too uncertain." People waiting for the "dip" in 2012 missed a 300% run. The best time to invest was yesterday. The second best is today.

Fix: Start a SIP this week. Set it up before you overthink it. You can always adjust amounts later.
Mistake #2

Buying Stocks Based on WhatsApp Tips

"Guaranteed 40% return in 6 months" — if this were real, the person sending it wouldn't share it on WhatsApp. Stock tips from friends, influencers, and Telegram groups have cost Indian retail investors tens of thousands of crore.

Fix: Buy index funds. You'll beat 90% of "tip followers" over 10 years.
Mistake #3

Stopping SIP When Markets Fall

This is the most common and most expensive mistake. When Nifty falls 20%, many investors stop their SIP — precisely when they should be buying more units at lower prices. They then restart when markets recover (= buying high again).

Fix: SIP mandates should be set and forgotten. A falling market = sale price on units. Keep buying.
Mistake #4

Too Many Funds = No Benefit

Having 15 mutual funds doesn't diversify more than 5 funds — they all own Reliance, HDFC, TCS, Infosys. What you get is 3× the complexity and tracking burden with zero extra diversification.

Fix: 3–4 well-chosen funds maximum. One large cap/index, one mid cap, one ELSS if needed.
Mistake #5

Confusing Insurance with Investment

Endowment plans and ULIPs are sold as "insurance + investment." The truth: they're bad insurance (cover is too low) and bad investments (returns 4–6% IRR over 20 years). A dual purpose product often does both poorly.

Fix: Term insurance (pure protection) + ELSS/Index SIP (pure investment). Never mix.
Mistake #6

Regular Plan Instead of Direct Plan

Regular mutual fund plans pay 0.5–1.5% annual commission to the distributor. Over 20 years on a ₹10L investment at 12% CAGR, this difference costs ₹22 lakh — nearly 2.5× your initial investment, silently drained year by year.

Fix: Always invest via Direct Plan. MFCentral, Zerodha Coin, Groww, or AMC website.
Mistake #7

Checking Portfolio Every Day

Daily portfolio checking leads to emotional decisions. On a bad day, you'll want to sell everything. On a good day, you'll feel invincible and take too much risk. The market is designed to transfer wealth from anxious people to patient people.

Fix: Check portfolio monthly at most. Annual review for rebalancing.
Mistake #8

No Emergency Fund Before Investing

Investing ₹10,000/month with zero emergency fund means any unexpected expense — medical bill, job loss, car repair — forces you to redeem your investments, often at a loss at the worst time.

Fix: Build 3–6 months expense emergency fund first. Then invest aggressively.
Mistake #9

Chasing Last Year's Top Fund

The fund with the highest 1-year return is usually a sector/thematic fund that had one great year. Investors pile in, the sector mean-reverts, and they're left holding a poorly performing fund they bought at the top.

Fix: Evaluate 5-year and 10-year consistent performance. Look at rolling returns vs benchmark, not absolute returns.
Mistake #10

Not Increasing SIP as Income Grows

Someone starts ₹5,000/month SIP at 25 and never increases it — even when their salary goes from ₹40,000 to ₹2,00,000 over 15 years. The lifestyle inflates; the savings rate deflates.

Fix: Increase SIP by 10% every year. One reminder on April 1 each year. Automate if possible.
