Last-Minute Tax Saving Tips Before March 31

It's March, your HR is screaming for investment proofs, and you've done nothing. Don't panic — here are 7 actions you can still take in the next few weeks to legally save ₹30,000–78,000 in taxes.

Act Fast — These Instruments Accept Last-Minute Investment

1
ELSS Mutual Fund (Lump Sum) — Instant

Invest a lump sum in any ELSS fund today via Groww, Zerodha Coin, or MFCentral. Units allotted same day. Submit proof immediately. Max ₹1.5L qualifies under 80C.

Save: ₹30,000–46,800 tax (depending on your bracket)
2
PPF — Online Transfer

If you have an existing PPF account, transfer up to ₹1.5L via net banking. Instant credit. 7.1% interest, fully tax-free. Combine with ELSS only if you want guaranteed returns portion.

Save: Up to ₹46,800 under 80C
3
NPS Tier 1 — 80CCD(1B) Extra ₹50,000

Contribute to NPS Tier 1 for an ADDITIONAL ₹50,000 deduction over and above 80C. Online via NPS website or bank in under 10 minutes. This is separate from the ₹1.5L 80C limit.

Save: Extra ₹15,600 (30% bracket)
4
Health Insurance Premium — Section 80D

Buy or renew health insurance online (Niva Bupa, Bajaj Allianz, HDFC Ergo). Up to ₹25,000 for self + family, ₹50,000 for senior citizen parents. Policy effective instantly.

Save: Up to ₹7,500–22,500 under 80D
5
Home Loan Interest — Section 24(b)

If you have a home loan, claim up to ₹2 lakh interest paid this financial year. Get the interest certificate from your bank before March 31 — you'll need it for IT return.

Save: Up to ₹60,000 (30% bracket)
6
HRA Exemption — If Not Already Claimed

If you pay rent but haven't submitted rent receipts/agreement to employer, collect them now. HRA exemption is massive for metro residents — often ₹1–2L+ per year tax-free.

Save: Varies — potentially ₹30,000–60,000+
7
NSC / 5-Year Bank FD — For Risk-Averse

5-year FD at any bank or Post Office NSC qualifies under 80C. Lower returns than ELSS but guaranteed. Complete investment by March 31 — interest earned also qualifies year 1.

Save: Part of ₹1.5L 80C bucket

What to Avoid in Last-Minute Rush

DON'T buy LIC/endowment policies. Agents know January–March is their harvest season — they push expensive, commission-heavy policies to people panicking about taxes. ELSS gives better returns AND similar tax benefits.
DON'T buy ULIP. 5-year minimum, high charges (3–4% annually in early years), poor liquidity. Tax saving doesn't justify the terrible economics.

The Rule Going Forward

Start next financial year's tax saving in April. Set up a monthly ELSS SIP of ₹12,500 per month — by March, you've automatically completed your full ₹1.5L 80C contribution without any panic.

Smart approach: Systematic SIP investment throughout the year gives better returns than lump-sum in March (due to rupee cost averaging) AND eliminates last-minute stress forever.
