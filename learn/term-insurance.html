Term Insurance — The One Insurance You Must Have

For ₹10,000–15,000/year, you can protect ₹1 crore for your family. Term insurance is the most important and most underused financial product in India. Here's everything.

What is Term Insurance?

Term insurance provides a lump-sum payout (death benefit) to your nominees if you die during the policy term. No investment component, no maturity benefit — just pure, affordable life protection.

❌ Endowment/ULIP

Mixes insurance + investment. Premium ₹50,000–3,00,000/year for ₹1 Cr cover. Returns: 4–6% IRR. Very poor value.

✅ Term Insurance

Pure protection only. Premium ₹8,000–15,000/year for ₹1 Cr cover (30-yr-old, non-smoker). Full cover, nothing wasted.

How Much Cover Do You Need?

A common rule: cover = 10–20× your annual income. But a better formula considers your liabilities and dependents:

  • Annual income × 15 years (to replace income till retirement)
  • + All outstanding loans (home loan, car loan, personal loan)
  • + Children's education costs
  • − Existing savings/investments already in place
Example Calculation

Annual income: ₹10L. Income replacement (15 yrs): ₹1.5 Cr. Home loan: ₹40L. Children's education: ₹20L. Existing assets: ₹10L.
Ideal cover = ₹1.5 Cr + ₹40L + ₹20L − ₹10L = ₹2 Crore

Minimum: ₹1 Crore cover. If you earn ₹10L+ per year, aim for ₹2 Crore. The premium difference is surprisingly small — ₹5,000–8,000/year more for double the cover.

When & How to Buy

  • Best age to buy: 25–30. Premiums increase significantly after 35.
  • Policy duration: Till age 60–65 (till you retire and dependents are independent)
  • Claim Settlement Ratio: Choose insurers with 97%+ CSR (check IRDAI annual report)
  • Buy online directly: Policybazaar, insurer website — same policy, lower premium
  • Disclose everything: Pre-existing conditions, smoking habits — non-disclosure = claim rejection

What to Avoid

Return of Premium Plans: Pay 2–3× more premium to get back premiums at maturity. The IRR is terrible — ~4%. Invest the extra premium in a SIP instead — you'll end up with far more.
Buying from bank/agent: Agents earn 20–35% commission in Year 1 and are incentivized to sell endowment products, not term insurance.
Waiting: Every year of delay increases your premium. A 25-year-old pays 30–40% less than a 35-year-old for identical cover.
