The Power of Compounding — Einstein's 8th Wonder

Compound interest is the most powerful force in wealth creation. Understand why starting 5 years earlier can mean the difference between ₹50 lakh and ₹1 crore at retirement.

What is Compounding?

Compounding means your investment returns also earn returns. Unlike simple interest (which grows linearly), compound interest grows exponentially — slowly at first, then explosively over time.

Formula: Future Value = Principal × (1 + rate)^years

Simple vs Compound Interest on ₹1,00,000 at 12%

Simple Interest (20 years): ₹1L + ₹2.4L interest = ₹3.4L total
Compound Interest (20 years): ₹1L grows to ₹9.65L — nearly 3× more!
The extra ₹6.25L is entirely from compounding — you invested nothing extra.

Rule of 72 — Mental Math for Compounding

The Rule of 72 tells you how many years it takes to double your money: 72 ÷ Annual Return = Years to Double

  • Savings account (4%): 72 ÷ 4 = 18 years to double
  • Fixed deposit (7%): 72 ÷ 7 = ~10 years to double
  • Equity MF (12%): 72 ÷ 12 = 6 years to double
  • Credit card debt (36%): 72 ÷ 36 = 2 years your DEBT doubles

What Kills Compounding

  • Withdrawing early: Breaking compounding resets the clock entirely
  • High expenses: 1.5% extra annual fee reduces your 30-year wealth by 30%
  • Pausing SIPs: Even 6 months of missed SIPs can cost lakhs long-term
  • Inflation ignorance: 6% inflation halves purchasing power in 12 years
