Health Insurance — Protect Your Wealth

One medical emergency without insurance can wipe out years of savings. Medical inflation in India is 12–14% annually — a ₹5L hospitalisation today will cost ₹20L+ in 10 years. Here's how to protect yourself.

Why Health Insurance is Non-Negotiable

Healthcare costs in India's private hospitals have risen 4× in the past decade. A serious illness (cancer, cardiac surgery, kidney failure) can cost ₹10–50 lakh. Without insurance, most families either deplete savings or go into debt.

Group insurance from employer is not enough. It covers only you (often), lapses the day you quit, and typically has ₹3–5L coverage — inadequate for major illnesses. You need personal health insurance.

How Much Coverage Do You Need?

  • Individual (under 35, metro city): Minimum ₹10 lakh base plan
  • Family floater (2 adults + kids): ₹15–25 lakh
  • With senior parents: Separate ₹10L policy for parents (family floater becomes expensive)
  • Super top-up: Add a ₹50–1 Crore super top-up above the base for very low premium
Pro strategy: ₹5L base plan + ₹45L super top-up (deductible ₹5L). Total coverage ₹50L for ~30–40% less premium than a direct ₹50L plan. Super top-up kicks in after base is exhausted.

What to Check Before Buying

✅
Claim Settlement RatioLook for 95%+ CSR (IRDAI publishes this annually). Low CSR = claims often rejected.
✅
Cashless Hospital NetworkCheck if your preferred hospitals are in the insurer's network. No-cost list available on insurer website.
✅
Waiting PeriodPre-existing diseases: 2–4 year waiting period. Maternity: 2–3 years. Buy early while healthy.
✅
Room Rent LimitAvoid sub-limits on room rent (e.g., 1% of sum assured). These proportionally reduce all claim amounts.
✅
No Co-Payment ClauseAvoid plans requiring you to pay 10–20% of each claim (common in senior citizen plans).

Common Health Insurance Mistakes

Depending only on employer cover: Your cover ends when employment ends. During job transitions, you're exposed — exactly when stress-related health issues spike.
Buying the cheapest plan: Cheap plans often have low sub-limits, high co-pays, and narrow hospital networks. Claim reimbursement becomes a nightmare.
Not disclosing pre-existing conditions: Non-disclosure leads to claim rejection when you need it most. Always disclose honestly, even if it raises premium.
