Systematic Investment Plan Explained

India's most powerful wealth-building tool — how ₹5,000/month invested consistently can grow into over ₹1.76 crore in 30 years, without timing the market.

What is a SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) allows you to invest a fixed amount in a mutual fund at regular intervals — usually monthly. Think of it like an EMI, but instead of paying off a debt, you're building wealth.

When you start a SIP, a fixed amount is automatically debited from your bank account on a chosen date every month, and units of the selected mutual fund are allocated to you at the prevailing NAV (Net Asset Value) price.

Key Insight: SIP doesn't require you to time the market. You invest the same amount every month — whether the market is up or down. This is called Rupee Cost Averaging.

How Rupee Cost Averaging Works

When markets fall, your fixed SIP amount buys more units. When markets rise, it buys fewer units. Over time, your average cost per unit reduces — giving you better returns than a lump-sum investor who tried to time the market.

Example

You invest ₹5,000/month. January: NAV ₹100 → 50 units. February: NAV ₹80 → 62.5 units. March: NAV ₹110 → 45.45 units. Average cost: ₹96.7 vs current NAV ₹110. You're already in profit even though the market went down first.

Benefits of SIP

  • Start small: Begin with as little as ₹500/month
  • Disciplined investing: Auto-debit removes the temptation to skip
  • No market timing needed: Rupee cost averaging handles volatility
  • Flexible: Pause, increase, or stop anytime
  • Tax efficient: ELSS SIPs save tax under Section 80C
  • Transparent: Daily NAV, SEBI regulated, audited funds

Common SIP Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Stopping SIP when markets fall. This is the worst time to stop — you're actually buying more units at lower prices. Stopping locks in losses and breaks compounding.
Mistake #2: Too many funds. 3–4 well-chosen funds are better than 15 overlapping ones. Diversification within a fund is already built in.
Mistake #3: Choosing regular plan over direct. Regular plans pay 0.5–1.5% commission to agents annually. Over 20 years, this can cost you ₹15–25 lakh on a modest SIP.
Mistake #4: Ignoring step-up SIP. Increase your SIP by 10% each year as your salary grows. ₹5,000/month stepped up to 10% annually for 20 years generates 2.5× more than a flat SIP.

How to Start Your First SIP

  • Step 1: Complete KYC via Aadhaar + PAN (takes 10 minutes online)
  • Step 2: Choose a platform — Zerodha Coin, Groww, MFCentral, or your bank
  • Step 3: Select a fund — for beginners, start with a Nifty 50 Index Fund
  • Step 4: Set your SIP date, amount, and bank mandate
  • Step 5: Never touch it for at least 5 years. Let compounding work.
Best First Fund: Nifty 50 Index Fund (Direct Plan) from any major AMC. Low expense ratio (0.1–0.2%), fully diversified, market-beating over most 10-year periods.
