Calculate Your Retirement Corpus

Your retirement corpus is the amount needed to fund all your expenses from retirement day until the end of your life — adjusted for inflation.

Corpus Calculation Example

Monthly expenses today: ₹60,000. Retire at 60, live till 85 (25 years). Inflation: 6%. Real return on corpus: 8%.
Inflation-adjusted monthly expense at 60: ₹60,000 × (1.06)^30 = ₹3.44L/month
Required corpus: ≈ ₹4.6 Crore (using 4% withdrawal rate)
Monthly SIP needed (30 yrs, 12% CAGR): ₹14,000/month

The 4% Rule

The 4% rule says you can withdraw 4% of your corpus annually without running out of money for 30 years. This means you need: Annual expenses ÷ 4% = Corpus needed

  • Need ₹6L/year in retirement → Corpus needed: ₹1.5 Crore
  • Need ₹12L/year in retirement → Corpus needed: ₹3 Crore
  • Need ₹24L/year in retirement → Corpus needed: ₹6 Crore

Note: For India, where inflation averages 6%, the safe withdrawal rate is closer to 3–3.5% to be conservative.

Mistakes That Destroy Retirement Plans

  • Starting too late: Every 5-year delay requires doubling your monthly savings
  • Ignoring inflation: Planning at today's prices severely underestimates future needs
  • No equity post-retirement: A 60-70 year old with 30 more years still needs growth assets
  • Over-relying on children: Build your own corpus — don't plan to be a burden
  • No healthcare fund: Medical costs in India are rising 12–14% annually
