Credit Cards — Use Smart, Build Wealth

Used correctly, a credit card is a free 45-day loan, a reward machine, and a credit score builder. Used wrongly, it's a 36–42% interest trap that destroys financial health.

The Interest Trap — How Bad It Really Is

Indian credit cards charge 36–42% annual interest (3–3.5%/month). This is the most expensive money you'll ever borrow. On a ₹50,000 balance, you pay ₹1,750/month in interest alone — without reducing the principal.
Minimum Payment Trap

Balance: ₹1,00,000. Minimum payment (5%): ₹5,000/month. Interest rate: 3.5%/month.
Time to pay off at minimum payments: 7+ years. Total paid: ₹2,40,000+ on a ₹1,00,000 bill.
You paid 2.4× the original amount. That's the trap.

The Golden Rules of Credit Card Use

  • Rule 1: Pay the FULL bill every month, never the minimum
  • Rule 2: Never spend more than you have in your bank account
  • Rule 3: Use it for regular expenses (groceries, fuel) — not for new purchases you can't afford
  • Rule 4: Keep credit utilization below 30% of your limit
  • Rule 5: Set auto-pay for the full amount due — never miss a payment
The ideal credit card user: Spends only what they'd spend anyway, earns 1–5% cashback/reward, pays full bill on due date, and pays ₹0 in interest — ever.

Credit Score — Why It Matters

Your CIBIL score (300–900) determines your eligibility for home loans, car loans, and personal loans — and the interest rate you get. A score above 750 is "excellent."

  • Pay on time: Payment history is 35% of your score
  • Low utilization: Keep usage below 30% of credit limit
  • Age of accounts: Older cards with good history help
  • Don't apply for too many cards: Each application triggers a hard inquiry
Home loan tip: CIBIL score of 750+ gets you 0.25–0.5% lower interest rate. On a ₹50L, 30-year home loan, this saves ₹7–15 lakh total. Your credit card behavior directly impacts this.
