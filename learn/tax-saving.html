Tax Saving Guide — Save Up to ₹78,000 This Year

Legal tax saving strategies using Section 80C, ELSS, HRA, NPS, and more. Most salaried Indians leave ₹30,000–₹78,000 in tax savings unclaimed every year.

Section 80C — ₹1.5 Lakh Deduction

Section 80C allows you to deduct up to ₹1.5 lakh from your taxable income every year. Tax saved depends on your bracket:

Tax Bracket80C Deduction (₹1.5L)Tax Saved
20% slab₹1,50,000₹30,000
30% slab₹1,50,000₹45,000
30% + 4% cess₹1,50,000₹46,800

Best 80C instruments (ranked by return):

  • ELSS Mutual Fund (3-yr lock-in): 12–15% historical CAGR — highest return of all 80C options
  • EPF (Employee Provident Fund): 8.15% tax-free — government guaranteed
  • PPF (15-year): 7.1% tax-free — very safe, sovereign guarantee
  • NPS Tier 1: Market-linked + extra ₹50,000 deduction under 80CCD(1B)
  • Avoid: LIC endowment, ULIP — 4–5% IRR, poor value

HRA — Most Missed Tax Benefit

If you pay rent and receive HRA from your employer, you can claim exemption on a portion of it. Many salaried employees never claim this.

HRA Example

Basic salary: ₹50,000/month. HRA received: ₹25,000/month. Rent paid: ₹22,000/month.
Exempt HRA = Min(₹25,000, ₹17,000, ₹25,000) = ₹17,000/month = ₹2.04L/year
At 30% bracket: Tax saving = ₹61,200/year

Section 80D — Health Insurance Deduction

  • Self + family under 60: Deduction up to ₹25,000/year on health insurance premium
  • Senior citizen parents: Additional ₹50,000/year deduction on their health insurance
  • Both together: Up to ₹75,000 deduction possible, saving ₹22,500 in taxes

NPS — Extra ₹50,000 Over 80C

Section 80CCD(1B) allows an additional ₹50,000 deduction for NPS contributions — over and above the ₹1.5L 80C limit. At 30% bracket, this saves an extra ₹15,600/year.

Total tax-efficient deductions possible: ₹1.5L (80C) + ₹50K (NPS 80CCD1B) + ₹25K (80D) + HRA exemption. Many Indians can legally reduce taxable income by ₹2.5–3L+ annually.
