Budgeting — The 50/30/20 Rule & More

A budget doesn't restrict your freedom — it gives you freedom. Master the systems that let you spend guilt-free while automatically building wealth every month.

The 50/30/20 Rule

Created by Senator Elizabeth Warren, this simple framework divides your after-tax income into three buckets:

50%
Needs

Rent, groceries, utilities, transport, EMIs, insurance

30%
Wants

Dining out, OTT subscriptions, shopping, hobbies, travel

20%
Savings

Emergency fund, SIPs, loan prepayment, investments

Example: ₹1,00,000/month take-home

Needs (50%): ₹50,000 — Rent ₹25,000, groceries ₹8,000, transport ₹5,000, EMIs ₹12,000
Wants (30%): ₹30,000 — Dining ₹8,000, shopping ₹10,000, entertainment ₹12,000
Savings (20%): ₹20,000 — SIP ₹15,000, emergency fund ₹5,000

Indian context: If you live in a metro city with high rent, your needs may be 60–65%. That's okay — adjust wants to 15–20% and maintain 20% savings. The savings % is non-negotiable.

Pay Yourself First — The Wealth Builder's Rule

Instead of saving what's left after spending, invest first — then live on the rest. Set up an automatic SIP on your salary date, before you can see or touch the money.

  • Salary hits account on 1st → SIP debits on 2nd
  • What's left = your spending budget — no guilt
  • You'll naturally adjust spending to what's available

Zero-Based Budgeting

Every rupee is assigned a "job" — Income minus all allocations = Zero. More rigorous than 50/30/20 but very powerful.

  • List all income sources for the month
  • Allocate every rupee: expenses, savings, investments, fun
  • If you have unallocated money, assign it to debt payoff or investments
  • Review at month end, adjust next month's budget
