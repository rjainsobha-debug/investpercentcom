Inflation — The Silent Destroyer of Your Savings

India's average inflation is 5–6% per year. That means your savings account earning 3.5% is losing real value every single year. Here's the math and what to do about it.

What is Inflation?

Inflation is the rate at which the general price level of goods and services rises over time — reducing the purchasing power of your money. When inflation is 6%, something that cost ₹100 last year costs ₹106 today.

India's CPI (Consumer Price Index) inflation has averaged 5–6% annually over the past decade. The RBI targets 4% inflation with a ±2% tolerance band.

Real Purchasing Power Example

In 2004, ₹100 bought ~4 litres of petrol. In 2024, it buys less than 1 litre. Your money's purchasing power dropped over 75% in 20 years — without you doing anything wrong. Inflation did it silently.

Types of Inflation

  • Demand-pull: Too much money chasing too few goods — economy overheating
  • Cost-push: Rising production costs (oil, wages) passed on to consumers
  • Built-in / Wage-price spiral: Workers demand higher wages → prices rise → more wage demands
  • Asset price inflation: Stocks, real estate, gold rising faster than consumer prices

How to Beat Inflation

Rule: Your investments must earn MORE than the inflation rate to grow your real wealth. At 6% inflation, you need at least 7%+ returns just to stand still.
  • Equity Mutual Funds: 12–15% CAGR historically — beats inflation by 6–9%
  • Gold: 8–10% CAGR — natural inflation hedge
  • Real Estate: 6–9% CAGR + rental yield — just barely beats inflation
  • Fixed Deposit (7%): Barely matches inflation — no real growth
  • Savings Account (3.5%): Loses to inflation every year
Beating Inflation with Equity

₹10L invested in equity mutual funds at 12% CAGR for 20 years = ₹96.5L
₹10L in savings account at 3.5% for 20 years = ₹19.9L
Meanwhile, inflation (6%) requires ₹32L to maintain purchasing power.
Equity wins. Savings account loses — dramatically.

Retirement & Inflation — The Hidden Risk

If you retire at 60 needing ₹50,000/month and inflation averages 6%, you'll need ₹1,60,000/month by age 80 to maintain the same lifestyle. Most retirement plans ignore this — don't let yours.

  • Always plan retirement corpus with inflation-adjusted expenses
  • Include equity allocation in your retirement portfolio even post-retirement
  • A pure FD/debt portfolio for retirement is a recipe for outliving your money
