Forex Card vs Cash vs Credit Card

  • Forex Card (recommended primary): Pre-loaded at good rates, secure (chip+PIN), widely accepted, card blocked if stolen without cash loss risk. Best for 80% of spending.
  • Foreign Currency Cash: Carry 10–15% of budget in cash for small vendors, taxis, tips. Not more — loss/theft risk.
  • Travel Credit Card (backup): Use no-forex-markup cards like Scapia, Niyo Global for emergencies. Avoid regular credit cards abroad (1.5–3.5% forex markup).
Best combo: Load 70% on forex card + keep 15% as cash + keep 15% credit limit on a zero-markup travel card as backup. This gives you security + flexibility.

Forex Card Comparison

  • HDFC Multicurrency Platinum Card: 22 currencies, ₹500 issuance fee, cashback on reload
  • Thomas Cook One Currency Card: USD only, zero transaction fees, good for USA trips
  • BookMyForex Prepaid Card: Rate locked at booking, no hidden charges
  • Niyo Global: Zero forex markup, load via UPI, best for frequent travellers
Leftover currency: Reconvert forex card balance before it expires — idle foreign currency in a card earns nothing. Some cards charge monthly fee on unused balances.
