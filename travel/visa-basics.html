Visa Types & Costs — Plan Before You Apply

Indian passport holders need visas for most countries. Knowing the costs, processing times, and financial requirements in advance helps you budget accurately and avoid nasty surprises.

Common Visa Types

  • Tourist Visa: For leisure travel. Most common. 15 days to 10 years validity depending on country.
  • Business Visa: For meetings, conferences. May require employer letter and client invite.
  • Student Visa: For study abroad. Requires university admission letter, financial proof, language test scores.
  • Work Visa: For employment abroad. Requires employer sponsorship. Most complex to obtain.
  • e-Visa: Online application, no embassy visit. Available for Thailand, UAE, Sri Lanka, USA ESTA, UK ETA, etc.
  • Visa on Arrival: Obtained at the destination airport. Available for ~50 countries for Indian passport holders.

Financial Requirements for Visa Applications

Most countries require proof that you can fund your trip and return. Common requirements:

  • Bank statements: 3–6 months showing adequate balance (Schengen: typically ₹50,000–1L/week of travel)
  • ITR (Income Tax Return): Last 2 years — proof of income
  • Salary slips: Last 3–6 months
  • Leave letter: From employer proving you'll return
  • Return ticket + hotel bookings: Required before visa, refundable bookings are acceptable for most countries
Schengen Tip: Apply through the country where you'll spend the most nights. If visiting multiple countries equally, apply through the first entry point. Rejection in one Schengen country affects future applications across all Schengen states.

  • Mauritius (90 days), Seychelles (30 days), Fiji, Jamaica, Trinidad
  • Many Caribbean, Pacific island nations

Always verify: Visa policies change frequently. Check the official embassy website or the Indian MEA passport visa advisor portal before booking flights. Never rely solely on blogs or travel influencers.
