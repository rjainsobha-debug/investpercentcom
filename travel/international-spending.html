International Spending — Cards vs Cash Strategy

Using a regular Indian credit card abroad can cost you an extra 3.5% on every transaction. With the right setup, you can spend internationally at near-interbank rates.

Ranked: Best Ways to Spend Money Abroad

1
Niyo Global / Scapia Card — Zero Forex MarkupLoad INR, spend in any currency at live interbank rate. Zero forex markup. 2–5% cashback on international spending. Best for frequent travellers.
2
Forex Prepaid Card (Thomas Cook, HDFC, BookMyForex)Lock rate in India, spend abroad at locked rate. No per-transaction fees in most. Best for budget certainty.
3
Local Currency Cash (15–20% of budget)Essential for local markets, taxis, tips. Get from forex dealer in India — not airport. Keep ₹50,000 equivalent max in cash.
4
International Travel Credit Card (Amex, HDFC Regalia)Use only if it has low or zero forex markup. Good for emergencies and lounge access. Check your card's forex fee — typically 1.5–3.5%.
Avoid: Regular Indian debit/credit cards abroad. They add 1.5–3.5% forex markup + GST on markup + potentially ATM fees. On a ₹5 lakh trip, that's ₹17,500–25,000 wasted.

The DCC Trap — Never Say Yes

Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC) happens when a foreign ATM or POS terminal asks: "Would you like to pay in INR?" — Always say NO. Always pay in the local currency.

  • DCC adds 5–7% on top of the already unfavorable rate
  • The terminal uses the worst possible conversion rate
  • Once you click "INR", the merchant bank profits — not you
  • Your own bank then converts again — you're converted twice
Rule: In a foreign country, ALWAYS choose to pay in the local currency on any card machine. No exceptions.

Using ATMs Abroad

  • Use ATMs in banks only — avoid standalone ATMs (higher fees, DCC pressure)
  • Niyo Global users: Use any ATM abroad with zero fee (up to limits)
  • Forex card ATM withdrawals: Check your card's ATM withdrawal fee (₹150–300 per withdrawal typically)
  • Withdraw in larger amounts: Fewer transactions = fewer fees
  • Decline ATM's offered rate: Same DCC trap applies — always choose local currency

Managing Your Travel Budget Abroad

  • Set daily limits on your forex/travel card via app
  • Keep a rough daily spend tracker (even a simple note)
  • Keep 20% buffer for unexpected costs (entry fees, weather changes, medical)
  • Notify your bank before travel so your card isn't blocked for "suspicious foreign activity"
  • Save receipts for big purchases for customs declaration on return
