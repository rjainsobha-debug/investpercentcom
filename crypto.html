Crypto Learning
4 TOPICS
What is Bitcoin? +
Bitcoin (BTC) is the world's first decentralized digital currency, created in 2009 by the pseudonymous Satoshi Nakamoto. It operates on a peer-to-peer network without any central authority. Bitcoin uses blockchain technology — a public ledger where all transactions are recorded transparently. There will only ever be 21 million BTC in existence, making it deflationary by design. It is often called "digital gold" due to its scarcity and store-of-value properties. Bitcoin is the most liquid and widely adopted cryptocurrency globally.
What is Blockchain? +
A blockchain is a distributed, immutable ledger that records transactions across a network of computers. Each "block" contains a set of transactions and is cryptographically linked to the previous block, forming a "chain." Once data is written to a blockchain, it cannot be altered without the consensus of the network — making it highly secure and tamper-proof. Blockchain powers not only cryptocurrencies but also smart contracts, NFTs, DeFi protocols, supply chain tracking, and more. It eliminates the need for trusted intermediaries like banks in many financial processes.
What is Staking? +
Staking is the process of locking up your cryptocurrency in a blockchain network to support its operations — such as validating transactions — in exchange for rewards. It is primarily used in Proof-of-Stake (PoS) blockchains like Ethereum 2.0, Cardano, Solana, and Polkadot. When you stake, you "delegate" your coins to a validator node. In return, you earn staking rewards (similar to interest), typically ranging from 4% to 20% APY depending on the network. Staking is generally safer than mining and does not require expensive hardware. However, staked assets may be "locked" for a period and carry market risk.
What are the Risks of Crypto Investing? +
Crypto investing carries significant risks that all investors must understand:

Volatility: Prices can drop 50–90% in bear markets within weeks.
Regulatory Risk: Governments can impose bans, taxes, or restrictions at any time.
Security Risk: Hacks, phishing, and wallet theft are common. Always use hardware wallets.
Liquidity Risk: Small altcoins may be hard to sell at fair prices.
Scam Risk: Rug pulls, Ponzi schemes, and fake projects are widespread in DeFi.
Tax Risk: In India, crypto profits are taxed at a flat 30% + 1% TDS.

Never invest more than you can afford to lose. Diversify, DYOR (Do Your Own Research), and consider a long time horizon.
